## What Gets Removed

Standard items accepted in Beaverton cleanouts:

- Furniture — couches, beds, dressers, tables, chairs, shelving
- Appliances — refrigerators, washers, dryers, dishwashers, freezers
- Mattresses — all sizes
- Electronics — TVs, computers, monitors (e-waste sorted separately)
- Yard equipment — mowers, trimmers, patio furniture, garden tools
- General debris — boxes, clothing piles, miscellaneous household items
- Construction debris — small renovation leftovers, drywall, lumber scraps
- Scrap metal — sorted for recycling

**Not accepted:** Paint cans, propane tanks, chemicals, automotive fluids, 
and other hazardous materials. These require Washington County HHW drop-off.

## FAQ

### Does the crew sort items during the cleanout?

Yes. Items that can be donated get separated from general disposal. 
Metals are pulled for recycling. Electronics go to e-waste channels. 
You don't need to pre-sort — just flag anything you want to keep.

### How long does a typical Beaverton cleanout take?

Single room: 1–2 hours. Garage: 2–4 hours. Full house or estate: 
half day to full day depending on volume and accessibility.

### Do prices include disposal fees?

Yes. The quoted price covers labor, haul-away, and disposal. 
No hidden dump fees added at the end.

### Can a cleanout be scheduled for the same week?

For most standard volume jobs in Washington County, yes. 
Tight timelines are worth mentioning when you call — scheduling 
adjusts based on crew availability.
